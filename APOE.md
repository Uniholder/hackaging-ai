## 🔬 2. Structure and Functional Domains
- **Protein Length:** 317 amino acids (canonical sequence)
- **Key Domains / Motifs:**
  - Signal peptide (1–18)
  - LDL receptor-binding region (158–168)
  - Lipid-binding domain (210–290)
  - Homooligomerization domain (266–317)
  - Heparin-binding sites (162–165, 229–236)
  - 8 tandem repeats (80–255) for lipid interaction
- **Functional Roles:** Mediates lipoprotein metabolism, cholesterol transport, receptor-mediated endocytosis, and amyloid-beta clearance.
- **Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs):**
  - Phosphorylation at S147
  - O-linked glycosylation at T26, T36, T212, T307, S308, S314
  - N-linked glycation at K93 (in hyperglycemia)
  - Methionine sulfoxide at M143 (oxidation)
  - C-terminal proteolytic processing in neurons
- **Orthologs / Paralogs:** Highly conserved in mammals; murine *Apoe* shares >70% sequence identity. No direct invertebrate orthologs, but functional analogs exist in lipid metabolism pathways.

## 🧠 4. Pathways and Functional Networks
- **KEGG Pathways:**
  - [Alzheimer disease (hsa05010)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa05010): APOE4 promotes amyloid-beta aggregation and neuroinflammation.
  - [Cholesterol metabolism (hsa04979)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04979): Central role in reverse cholesterol transport and lipoprotein remodeling.
- **Interaction Partners:** LDLR, LRP1, LRP2, LRP8, VLDLR, heparan sulfate proteoglycans.
- **Functional Roles:** Regulates lipid homeostasis, amyloid-beta clearance, and neuroinflammatory responses. Dysregulation contributes to atherosclerosis and neurodegeneration.

## 🧓 5. Longevity and Aging Associations
- **Human Longevity Associations:**
  - **APOE*4 (ε4, rs429358):** Strongly associated with reduced lifespan, increased Alzheimer’s risk, and cardiovascular disease.
  - **APOE*2 (ε2, rs7412):** Linked to extended longevity and reduced Alzheimer’s risk in some populations.
- **Model Organism Data:**

| Model | Intervention | Result | Reference |
|--------|--------------|--------|------------|
| Mouse (*Apoe*<sup>-/-</sup>) | Gene knockout | ↓ Lifespan, dyslipidemia, atherosclerosis | [OpenGenes](https://opengenes.org) |
| Human (ε4 carriers) | Natural variant | ↓ Survival beyond age 85, ↑ Alzheimer’s incidence | [PMID: 10.1038/s41467-019-11558-2](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-019-11558-2) |

- **Key SNPs:**
  - rs429358 (ε4): Reduced odds of surviving to 90th/99th percentile age.
  - rs7412 (ε2): Increased odds of extreme longevity.

## 💊 6. Small Molecule and Drug Interactions
- **Heparin:** Binds to heparin-binding sites (162–165, 229–236), facilitating cellular uptake.
- **Lipids:** Critical for chylomicron, VLDL, and HDL association; lipidation status determines functional efficacy.
- **Glucose:** N-linked glycation at K93 in hyperglycemia impairs function.
- **Therapeutic Context:** No direct pharmacological modulators documented in KEGG/UniProt, but lipid-lowering therapies (e.g., statins) indirectly target APOE-mediated pathways.

---

## 🌍 7. Evolutionary Conservation
- **Mammalian Conservation:** APOE is highly conserved across mammals (>90% sequence identity in primates, >70% in rodents).
- **Functional Conservation:** Murine *Apoe* knockout recapitulates human dyslipidemia and atherosclerosis. No direct invertebrate orthologs, but lipid transport mechanisms are evolutionarily ancient.
- **Longevity Link:** APOE*4’s detrimental effects on lifespan are conserved in human populations but not fully modeled in short-lived species.
