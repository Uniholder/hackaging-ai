## 🔬 2. Structure and Functional Domains
- **Protein Length:** 394 amino acids (canonical isoform)
- **Key Domains / Motifs:**
  - G-alpha domain (positions 39–394)
  - GTP-binding motifs: G1 (42–55), G2 (196–204), G3 (219–228), G4 (288–295), G5 (364–369)
  - Disordered regions (1–23, 68–91)
- **Functional Roles:**
  - Activates adenylate cyclase via GPCR signaling (GO:0007189)
  - Regulates cAMP-dependent pathways critical for hormone response and cellular metabolism
- **Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs):**
  - ADP-ribosylation at R201 (cholera toxin-mediated constitutive activation)
  - N- and S-palmitoylation at G2/C3 (membrane anchoring)
  - Phosphorylation at S352/S995 (regulatory)
  - Ubiquitination at K300 (degradation regulation)
- **Orthologs / Paralogs:**
  - Highly conserved Gαs subunits across vertebrates; no specific % identity provided in source data.

## 🧠 4. Pathways and Functional Networks
- **KEGG Pathways:**
  - [Dilated cardiomyopathy (hsa05414)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa05414): Regulates cardiac contractility via cAMP-dependent calcium handling.
  - [Endocrine resistance (hsa01522)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa01522): Mediates hormone resistance through dysregulated cAMP signaling in adrenal/thyroid pathways.
- **Interaction Partners:**
  - GPCRs (e.g., β-adrenergic receptors), adenylyl cyclase, KEAP1 (indirect via cAMP-PKA axis).
- **Biological Roles:** Central node in cAMP signaling cascades governing metabolism, cardiac function, and endocrine homeostasis.

---

## 🧓 5. Longevity and Aging Associations
- Constitutive activation (e.g., R201H/C) → elevated cAMP/PKA → increased cell proliferation and tumorigenesis → **reduced lifespan**.
- Loss-of-function variants (e.g., PHP1A/B/C) → impaired cAMP signaling → developmental defects and metabolic dysregulation → **reduced stress resilience**.
- Modulates cold-induced thermogenesis (GO:0120162), suggesting a role in metabolic aging pathways.

---

## 💊 6. Small Molecule and Drug Interactions
- **Cholera toxin:** ADP-ribosylates R201 → locks Gαs in active state → uncontrolled cAMP production.
- **Forskolin:** Indirect activator via adenylyl cyclase stimulation (cAMP pathway).
- **Pertussis toxin:** Inactivates Gαi (counter-regulatory pathway), indirectly enhancing Gαs signaling.

---

## 🌍 7. Evolutionary Conservation
- G-alpha domain universally conserved across eukaryotes (e.g., *S. cerevisiae* Gpa2, *D. melanogaster* Gαs).
- Critical residues (e.g., R201, GTP-binding motifs) invariant from mammals to invertebrates.
- Longevity-related cAMP signaling roles conserved in *C. elegans* (daf-2 pathway) and *D. melanogaster*.
