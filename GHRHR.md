## 🔬 2. Structure and Functional Domains
- **Protein Length:** 423 amino acids
- **Key Domains / Motifs:**
  - 7 transmembrane helices (positions 131–151, 168–188, 211–231, 241–261, 284–304, 332–352, 358–378)
  - Extracellular ligand-binding domain (positions 23–130)
  - C-terminal cytoplasmic tail (positions 379–423) for G-protein coupling
  - Disulfide bonds (41–64, 55–96, 78–112) stabilizing extracellular domain
- **Functional Roles:** G-protein coupled receptor (GPCR) mediating growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH) signaling to stimulate cAMP production and growth hormone secretion from pituitary somatotrophs.
- **Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs):**
  - N-linked glycosylation at position 50
  - Signal peptide cleavage (positions 1–22)
- **Orthologs / Paralogs:**
  - Mouse ortholog (*Ghrhr*) with loss-of-function mutation (lit/lit) causing dwarfism and lifespan extension
  - *C. elegans* and *Drosophila* lack direct orthologs but share conserved GH/IGF-1 signaling components

## 🧠 4. Pathways and Functional Networks
- **KEGG Pathways:**
  - [Neuroactive ligand-receptor interaction (hsa04080)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04080): Mediates GHRH binding to activate Gαs signaling and cAMP production.
  - [Growth hormone synthesis, secretion and action (hsa04935)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04935): Central role in GH release, intersecting with insulin/IGF-1, mTOR, and FOXO longevity pathways.
- **Interaction Partners:**
  - GHRH (ligand)
  - Gαs protein (activates adenylate cyclase)
  - Adenylate cyclase (cAMP production)
- **Biological Roles:** Regulates somatic growth, metabolism, and aging through GH/IGF-1 axis modulation.

## 💊 6. Small Molecule and Drug Interactions
- **Tesamorelin acetate:** Synthetic GHRH analog that activates GHRHR to stimulate GH secretion. Used for HIV-associated lipodystrophy, modulating GH/IGF-1 axis with implications for aging metabolism.
- **Mechanism:** Binds extracellular domain (positions 23–130), stabilizing active conformation to enhance Gαs coupling and cAMP production.
- **Pathway Link:** [Growth hormone synthesis pathway (hsa04935)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04935).

## 🌍 7. Evolutionary Conservation
- **Conserved Domains:** Transmembrane helices and disulfide-bonded extracellular domain preserved across mammals.
- **Orthologs:**
  - Mouse (*Ghrhr*): 85% amino acid identity; lit/lit mutation extends lifespan
  - Rat (*Ghrhr*): 83% identity; similar GH regulation
- **Functional Conservation:** GH/IGF-1 pathway's role in lifespan regulation conserved from invertebrates (*C. elegans* DAF-2 pathway) to mammals.
