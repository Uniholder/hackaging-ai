## 🔬 2. Structure and Functional Domains
- **Protein Length:** 340 amino acids (canonical isoform)
- **Key Domains / Motifs:** Seven WD40 repeats (positions 46–340), structural helix (3–25), beta strands, and turns
- **Functional Roles:** Mediates signal transduction from G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) to intracellular effectors; critical for Ras/MAPK pathway activation and cancer-related signaling
- **Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs):**
  - Ser-2: N-acetylation (stability) and phosphorylation
  - His-266: Phosphohistidine (enhances G protein activation via phosphate transfer)
- **Orthologs / Paralogs:** WD40 domains are evolutionarily conserved; no specific ortholog % identity data provided in sources

## 🧠 4. Pathways and Functional Networks
- **KEGG Pathways:**
  - [Ras signaling pathway (hsa04014)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04014): GNB1 transduces GPCR signals to activate Ras/MAPK cascades, influencing cell proliferation.
  - [Pathways in cancer (hsa05200)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa05200): Contributes to oncogenic signaling via GPCR-Ras crosstalk in tumor progression.
- **Interaction Partners:** Forms heterotrimers with Gα and Gγ subunits; interacts with GNAI2, GNAI3, GNA13, and GNA11 (UniProt variants data)

## 🌍 7. Evolutionary Conservation
WD40 repeat domains are highly conserved across eukaryotes, critical for structural stability and protein-protein interactions. Specific orthologs include *C. elegans* GPB-1 and *Drosophila* Gβ13F, though longevity-related functional conservation was not documented in provided data.
