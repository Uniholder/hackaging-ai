## 🔬 2. Structure and Functional Domains
- **Protein Length:** 638 amino acids (canonical isoform)
- **Key Domains / Motifs:**
  - Signal peptide (1–18)
  - Extracellular domain (19–264) containing fibronectin type-III domain (151–254) and WSXWS motif (240–244)
  - Transmembrane domain (265–288)
  - Cytoplasmic domain (289–638) with Box 1 motif (297–305) and UbE motif (340–349)
  - Disordered region (353–391)
- **Functional Roles:** Mediates growth hormone (GH) binding, JAK2 activation, and downstream STAT5 signaling for growth, metabolism, and cell proliferation.
- **Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs):**
  - N-linked glycosylation (N46, N115, N156, N161, N200)
  - Phosphorylation (S341, Y487, Y595)
  - Ubiquitination leading to receptor internalization and degradation
- **Orthologs / Paralogs:** Conserved in mammals; mouse ortholog shows identical domain architecture and functional roles in growth regulation.

## 🧠 4. Pathways and Functional Networks
- **KEGG Pathways:**
  - [Growth hormone synthesis, secretion and action (hsa04935)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04935): Central pathway for GH-mediated growth and metabolic regulation.
  - [Cytokine-cytokine receptor interaction (hsa04060)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04060): GHR functions as a cytokine receptor family member.
  - [JAK-STAT signaling pathway (hsa04630)](https://www.kegg.jp/pathway/hsa04630): Primary signaling cascade activated by GHR upon GH binding.
- **Interaction Partners:** JAK2 (kinase activation), STAT5 (transcription factor), SOCS2 (negative regulator via ubiquitination).

## 💊 6. Small Molecule and Drug Interactions
- **Lonapegsomatropin** ([KEGG D11486](https://www.kegg.jp/entry/D11486)): Long-acting GH analog that binds GHR, mimicking endogenous GH to stimulate growth in deficiency states.
- **Mechanism:** Activates JAK2-STAT5 signaling pathway upon receptor binding, promoting growth and metabolic effects.

---

## 🌍 7. Evolutionary Conservation
- WSXWS motif and Box 1 domain are highly conserved across vertebrates, indicating critical roles in receptor function.
- Functional conservation of GHR in growth regulation is evident from mouse models, where loss-of-function mutations recapitulate human Laron syndrome phenotypes and extend lifespan.
